Chatpate

Chana chatpat is a popular street food snack served throughout Nepal, and loved by tourists and locals a like. Consisting of dried ramen noodles, finely diced vegetables, chickpeas, and seasoned with chili powder and lemon juice, this snack is both cheap and full of flavor. Often sold for as little as 25NPR ($0.20), chana chatpat traditionally comes in a piece of rolled up paper with the corner of the ramen packet used as a spoon.

Chatpate ingredients:

  • chickpeas
  • green peas
  • red onion
  • green chilli
  • potato
  • tomato
  • cilantro
  • bombay mix
  • puffed rice
  • ramen noodles
  • peanuts
  • lemon juice
  • extra virgin olive oil
  • cumin
  • chilli powder

Chatpate recipe:

  1. Peel and boil the potato for about 20-25 minutes until soft. If using frozen peas, throw them in for the last 5 minutes.
  2. Finely dice the red onion, green chili, tomato, and potato.
  3. In a large bowl, add the red onion, green chili, tomato, potato, chickpeas, green peas, noodles, bombay mix, peanuts, and lemon juice and stir.
  4. Add the salt, cumin powder, chili powder, and stir again.
  5. Add the puffed rice and seasoning packet from the noodles and gently stir.
  6. Add a drizzle of olive oil and the chopped cilantro and gently stir again.
  7. Serve immediately.
